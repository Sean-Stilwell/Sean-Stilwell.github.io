About Me

I'm a masters student studying Computer Science at the University of Ottawa. My research focus is on Natural Language Processing, my undergraduate Honours Project focused on Named Entity Recognition for legal documents. I have two years of work experience in the Government of Canada performing a variety of roles, including geospatial and bioinformatics work. 

I've been programming for about 6 years, and I'm proficient in a variety of languages. I have experience in C#, C++, Java, and Python, and have also learned concurrent, logical, and functional programming using Golang, Prolog, and Racket. I also have some experience with Swift and Kotlin for mobile development. I have full stack development experience using React.js and PostgreSQL.

I'm fluently bilingual, having attained my DELF B2 certification from the Ministry of Education of France, as well as
passing the Second Language Certification Test at the University of Ottawa. I also speak some German after learning it during my undergraduate degree.
